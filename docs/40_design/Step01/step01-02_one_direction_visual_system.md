# 02. One-Direction Visual System

## Direction: "The Institutional Brief"
A restrained, typography-first approach that mimics high-end financial gazzetes and private research reports.

## 1. Typography (Final Stack)
- **Heading Stack**: `Georgia, "Times New Roman", Times, serif`
- **Body Stack**: `system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif` (Modern editorial hybrid)
- **Fallback**: System serif/sans default.
- **External Fonts**: None (System-first for reliability).
- **Size Logic**:
    - **H1**: 36px (2.4rem) Desktop / 28px (1.8rem) Mobile.
    - **H2**: 28px (1.8rem) Desktop / 22px (1.4rem) Mobile.
    - **H3**: 22px (1.4rem) Desktop / 18px (1.2rem) Mobile.
    - **Body**: 16px (1rem) / 1.8 line-height.
- **Hierarchy**: Bold weights (700-800) for H1-H3; Normal (400) for body.

## 2. Spacing Rhythm
- **Breathable Blocks**: Increase vertical spacing between major sections.
- **Measure**: Keep the reading column narrow (680px-720px) for optimal readability.

## 3. Background/Surface Logic
- **Primary**: Cream/Off-white (`#fffcf5` or similar) to reduce eye strain compared to pure white.
- **Surfaces**: Very light gray or darker cream for callout boxes.

## 4. Divider Logic
- **Hairline Rules**: Use 1px borders in `#eee` or `#ddd`.
- **Editorial Accents**: 3px top-border on the footer and single-post headers to "frame" the content.

## 5. Accent Logic
- **Gold (`#b36b00`)**: Reserved strictly for the most important "Actionable" or "Primary Pick" elements. Use sparingly to maintain luxury feel.

## 6. Badge Language
- **Design**: Small, all-caps, light background with subtle borders. 
- **Colors**: Desaturated versions of brand colors (e.g., very light gold for "Picks", light blue for "Analysis").

## 7. Source/Reference Box
- **Design**: Shift from "Box" to "Tabular" view. Use a dedicated background with a subtle "Source" label in a monospaced font.

## 8. Homepage & Archive
- **Tone**: Professional, news-feed style. Remove generic "Read more" buttons in favor of clean title links and subtle meta-data.

## Rationale
Macromalt is about **discipline and quiet authority**. This direction avoids the "SaaS/Start-up" clutter and the "Default Blog" amateurism by focusing on the quality of information through superior typography and spatial hierarchy.
